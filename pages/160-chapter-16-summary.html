Part IV. Distributed Systems

Chapter 16. Distributed Transactions and Consensus

Chapter 16 Summary: What We Learned

Page Topic Takeaway in One Sentence
151 Why local transactions are easier On one node, locks, WAL, and commit are straightforward. Across nodes, coordination introduces latency and failure modes.
152 Two-phase commit Phase 1 (prepare): all participants agree they can commit. Phase 2 (commit/abort): coordinator tells everyone the final decision.
153 Coordinator failure If the coordinator crashes after some participants prepared, those participants are stuck (in-doubt) until the coordinator recovers.
154 Consensus in simple terms Consensus means getting a group of nodes to agree on a single value, even if some nodes fail or the network partitions.
155 Quorums A majority (n/2 + 1) of nodes must agree for a decision to be durable — ensures overlap between any two quorums.
156 Raft and leader election Raft uses a strong leader, log replication, and randomized election timeouts to achieve understandable consensus.
157 What consensus solves Linearizable reads/writes, replicated state machines, distributed locking, and leader election — the building blocks of distributed systems.
158 What consensus does not solve Consensus does not handle byzantine (malicious) failures, does not make queries fast, and adds significant latency to every decision.
159 Cost of coordination Every consensus round requires multiple network round-trips — this is why distributed transactions are orders of magnitude slower than local ones.
Reveal: How do these ideas connect to the next chapter?
Chapter 16 gave you the tools for distributed coordination: two-phase commit, consensus algorithms, and quorums. But using these tools forces difficult tradeoffs. Chapter 17 explores consistency models — from linearizability (strongest, slowest) to eventual consistency (weakest, fastest). You will learn the CAP theorem, the cost of coordination, and how to choose the right consistency guarantee for each part of your application. Consensus is a hammer; Chapter 17 teaches you when to use a gentler tool.
