Part VI. Engineering Practice

Chapter 23. Designing for Performance

Chapter 23 Summary: What We Learned

Page Topic Takeaway in One Sentence
221 Latency, throughput, and concurrency Latency = time for one operation; throughput = operations per second; concurrency = number of simultaneous operations. Optimize for your bottleneck.
222 Read-heavy vs write-heavy workloads Read-heavy: optimize indexes, add read replicas, use caching. Write-heavy: optimize WAL, partition data, batch writes.
223 Access patterns first Design your schema and indexes around how your application actually queries the data — not around how the data looks.
224 Picking the right indexes Create indexes on columns used in WHERE, JOIN, and ORDER BY clauses. Avoid redundant indexes. Measure, don't guess.
225 Avoiding unnecessary joins Denormalize when reads are critical and writes are rare; use covering indexes to avoid table lookups.
226 Caching and invalidation Cache frequently read, rarely written data; invalidate or update the cache on write. The two hardest problems: naming and invalidation.
227 Batch work vs online work Move heavy computation (reports, backfills, aggregations) to batch windows; keep online transactions fast and predictable.
228 Measuring before tuning Use query logs, slow-query analyzers, and monitoring dashboards; optimize the queries that consume the most total time, not just the slowest.
229 Performance tradeoffs Indexes speed reads but slow writes. Caching adds complexity. Partitioning helps writes but complicates reads. No free lunch.
Reveal: How do these ideas connect to the next chapter?
Chapter 23 taught you to make databases fast. But a fast database that loses your data is worse than a slow one. Chapter 24 covers reliability: backups, point-in-time recovery, high availability through failover, and safe schema migrations. You will learn that an untested backup is not a backup at all, that chaos engineering reveals real failure modes, and that operational runbooks matter as much as architectural choices. Speed wins sprints; reliability wins marathons.
