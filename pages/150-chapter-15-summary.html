Part IV. Distributed Systems

Chapter 15. Replication

Chapter 15 Summary: What We Learned

Chapter Contents

Page Topic Takeaway in One Sentence
141 Leader-follower replication One leader handles writes; followers replicate the leader's state and serve reads — the most common replication topology.
142 Synchronous vs asynchronous replication Sync: follower confirms each write (safe but slow). Async: leader continues without confirmation (fast but risky).
143 Read replicas Dedicated read-only copies offload read traffic; applications must tolerate replication lag (stale reads).
144 Replication lag The delay between a write on the leader and its appearance on a follower — causes read-your-writes anomalies.
145 Failover and promotion When the leader fails, a follower is promoted; this requires detecting failure and ensuring the new leader has all committed writes.
146 Conflict handling In multi-leader or leaderless systems, concurrent writes to the same key create conflicts — resolved by last-write-wins, CRDTs, or application logic.
147 Multi-leader systems Multiple nodes accept writes; conflicts are inevitable — useful for multi-datacenter setups but complex to operate.
148 Replication logs Statement-based, row-based (WAL shipping), or logical (change data capture) — each trades fidelity for flexibility.
149 Operational pitfalls Split-brain (two leaders), data loss during failover, infinite replication lag — replication needs monitoring and practiced runbooks.
